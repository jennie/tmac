The Toronto Media Arts Centre, now in its second year of operating a 30,000 sq. ft. arts centre in West Queen West, has quickly established itself as a vital hub for events, exhibitions and educational programming that applies art and technology to create social and civic impact.

In our first year, we hosted 275 events and 30 exhibitions, festivals and major productions. We collaborated with 150 arts nonprofits, ad hoc community groups, and city-wide initiatives. Now, every inch of our galleries and event spaces is programmed to capacity on a daily basis.

We have reached the limits of what we can do in the space without our long-awaited cinema. It is essential to our cultural mandate and at the core of our collaborative programming vision.

The cinema space – 3,500 sq. ft., currently just a shell in the centre of our building – was designed by us to be the heart of all activity in TMAC and a hub for Toronto’s 100+ independent film festivals and the local neighbourhood.

Its design and programming respond to the needs and diverse imaginations of the 150+ partners we’ve worked with for presentation, theatre and musical performance, film screening, conferences and more.
